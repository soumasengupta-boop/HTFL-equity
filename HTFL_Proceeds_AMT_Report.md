## How AMT Credit Is Recovered

When ISO shares are sold in a **qualifying disposition** (held >1 year from exercise AND >2 years from grant), a negative AMT adjustment is generated equal to the original exercise-date spread:

| Lot | Shares | Spread/Share | Total Negative Adjustment |
|-----|--------|-------------|--------------------------|
| B ($2.22 → $32.88 FMV) | 9,258 | $30.66 | $283,809 |
| A ($8.33 → $31.50 FMV) | 1,680 | $23.17 | $38,926 |
| **Total** | **10,938** | | **$322,735** |

This adjustment reduces Alternative Minimum Taxable Income (AMTI), causing tentative minimum tax to fall below regular tax — allowing the AMT credit to offset regular taxes dollar-for-dollar.

**Key insight:** The negative AMT adjustment is fixed regardless of the sale price. Selling at $40 vs $32 generates the same AMT credit recovery — but higher proceeds.
