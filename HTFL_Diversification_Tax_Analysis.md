## Overview

This report estimates the tax impact of selling all 31,463 HTFL brokerage shares over 3 years (2026–2028), structured so every sale qualifies for long-term capital gains (LTCG) treatment. It integrates the AMT credit recovery from the separately structured 10b5-1 ISO share sale plan.

**Key result: The $99,902 AMT paid in 2025 is fully recovered, reducing the effective LTCG tax rate from 29.8% to 15.4%.**

## Why AMT Credits Matter Here

In 2025, exercising 10,938 ISO shares triggered **$322,776 in AMT preference income**, resulting in:
- Federal AMT paid: **$82,831**
- California AMT paid: **$17,071**
- **Total AMT paid: $99,902**

These amounts become **AMT credits** — dollar-for-dollar offsets against future regular income tax. The credits are unlocked in years when regular income tax exceeds tentative minimum tax. Selling the ISO shares as qualifying dispositions generates **negative AMT adjustments** that accelerate this recovery:

| Year | ISO Shares Sold | Negative AMT Adjustment | Effect |
|------|----------------:|------------------------:|--------|
| 2026 | 6,258 (Lot B @ $2.22) | -$191,870 | Drives tentative min tax well below regular tax |
| 2027 | 4,680 (Lot B+A) | -$130,906 | Recovers remaining credit |
| 2028 | 0 | $0 | All credits already recovered |

## Key Observations

**1. California is the biggest drag.**
At 9.3% flat on LTCG (no preferential rate), California accounts for 60% of the Year 3 tax bill. There is no planning lever to reduce this without changing state residency before selling.

**2. AMT recovery cuts the effective rate almost in half.**
Without the AMT credit, the effective rate is 29.8%. With it, 15.4%. The $99,902 paid in 2025 comes back fully by end of 2027.

**3. 2026 is the best year to sell.**
The combination of ISO negative AMT adjustments and AMT credits makes 2026 nearly tax-free on capital gains. Front-loading sales to 2026 (within LTCG constraints) maximizes after-tax proceeds.

**4. Pre-IPO lots carry the most gain.**
The 12,936 pre-IPO shares at avg cost $5.83 represent ~$315K of the $693K total gain — nearly half. Spreading these across all 3 years prevents a single-year spike into the 20% federal bracket.

**5. Nov-2025 RSU lot (274 shares) has a small loss.**
-$701 at $30. Selling these in the highest-gain year (2026 or 2027) offsets some gain. Small but a free tax benefit.

**6. This analysis covers brokerage shares only.**
The 26,659 vested options and future RSU distributions are not included. Exercising options in 2027–2028 will add to income and likely push more LTCG into the 20% federal bracket.
